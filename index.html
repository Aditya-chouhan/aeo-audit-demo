Live demo · real crawls, no API keys, no fabricated scores

Would ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity actually cite this page?

Search is moving from blue links to AI answers, and the SEO playbook that won on Google doesn't automatically win on an LLM. I audited 6 real, live company websites for AI-engine discoverability (AEO/GEO) using a two-layer methodology: 16 deterministic checks, plus 6 content-quality dimensions I scored myself by reading what a crawler actually pulls back.

aeo-audit-demo— Node crawler + Claude-judged scoring, real sites throughout
Scale note

6 sites, 8 pages each, is a demo-scale sample chosen for name recognition and methodology — not a market survey. The deterministic layer is fully reproducible; the intelligence layer is my judgment, disclosed with a rationale per score, not a hidden black box.

Why this, not another lead-gen demo

The rest of this portfolio is lead-gen and enrichment (signal scoring, account briefs, a SQL warehouse). This is a different skill: AEO/GEO — whether AI engines can find, parse, and trust a page enough to cite it. It's a genuinely current 2025-2026 GTM problem, not a repeat of the same demo with a new API.

Methodology — two layers, 50/50

Script: scripts/aeo-audit.mjs — adapted from OnVoyage AI's open-source audit-website-aeo skill (MIT license, attributed in the repo). Not written by me; my contribution is running it against real sites and judging the intelligence layer.

layer 1 — foundational (deterministic, 0-100)

16 pass/fail checks per site: title/meta quality, canonical tags, schema.org/JSON-LD, heading hierarchy, llms.txt, AI-bot access in robots.txt, RSS feeds, image alt coverage, and more. Same input, same score, every run.

layer 2 — intelligence (Claude-judged, 0-100)

The script surfaces up to 5 representative pages per site with real title/headings/schema/text-excerpt data. I read those excerpts and scored 6 dimensions (0-5 each: Answer Readiness, Quotability, Evidence Density, Content Depth, Freshness, Structural Clarity) the way an AI agent deciding whether to cite a source actually would — rationale written before each score, all in data/scores.json.

Final score = round(0.5 × foundational + 0.5 × intelligence), mapped to an A-F grade.

HubSpot excluded

HubSpot was attempted as a 7th site and its homepage crawl returned 0 pages under the script's own bot-detection — per the script's own instruction not to fabricate a score on a failed crawl, it was dropped rather than forced. Documented in data/scores.json and the repo README, not silently swapped for an easier target.
